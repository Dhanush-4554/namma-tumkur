Mandaragiri Betta

Mandaragiri or Basadi-betta is a Jain pilgrimage located on Mandaragiri Hill in Tumkur district in the Indian state of Karnataka.

Mandaragiri Hills is a popular tourist destination located in the Tumkur district of Karnataka, India. The hill is situated about 65 kilometers away from Bangalore and is a perfect weekend getaway for those seeking a peaceful and serene atmosphere. The hill is known for its scenic beauty, religious significance, and trekking opportunities. At the top of the hill, there is a temple dedicated to Lord Parshwanath, an important figure in Jainism. The temple is believed to have been built around the 12th century and attracts a large number of devotees every year. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The trek to the top of the hill is a popular activity among visitors. The trek is moderate and can be completed by people of all ages. The trek takes around 20 to 30 minutes, and the path is well-maintained, making it easy for visitors to reach the top. The view from the top is spectacular, and visitors can see the entire Tumkur district from the hilltop. Apart from the temple and trekking, Mandaragiri Hills is also known for its beautiful lakes. The two lakes, Bindiganavile and Karigudda, are located at the base of the hill and offer visitors an opportunity to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the region. The lakes are surrounded by lush greenery and are a popular spot for picnics and day trips. The hill is also home to several wildlife species, including monkeys, peacocks, and various types of birds. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll around the hill and observe these animals in their natural habitat. The hill is also covered with several types of trees, including neem, tamarind, and mango trees, among others. One of the unique features of Mandaragiri Hills is the 435 steps that lead up to the temple at the top of the hill. These steps are carved out of rock and are surrounded by greenery, making the climb a pleasant experience. Visitors can also find several small caves along the way that are believed to have been used by Jain monks in ancient times. Overall, Mandaragiri Hills is a great place to visit for those seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The hill offers visitors a chance to connect with nature, explore the region's rich history and culture, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. With its picturesque location, rich history, and tranquil atmosphere, Mandaragiri Hills is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Tumkur.
