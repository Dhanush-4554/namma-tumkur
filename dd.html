Devarayana Durga Hills

Devarayanadurga or DD Hills is a beautiful hill station located in the Tumkur district of Karnataka, India. The hill station is situated at an altitude of 3940 feet above sea level and is a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty, ancient temples, and trekking opportunities.

The hill station is named after two important temples situated on the hill - the Yoganarasimha temple and the Bhoganarasimha temple. The Yoganarasimha temple is dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an important figure in Hindu mythology, while the Bhoganarasimha temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temples are believed to have been built during the rule of the Cholas and the Hoysalas and attract a large number of devotees every year. Apart from the temples, DD Hills is also known for its scenic beauty and trekking opportunities. The hill station is surrounded by lush green forests and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll around the hill and observe the flora and fauna of the region, including several species of birds and butterflies. The trek to the top of the hill is a popular activity among visitors. The trek is moderate and can be completed by people of all ages. The trek takes around 30 to 45 minutes, and the path is well-maintained, making it easy for visitors to reach the top. The view from the top is spectacular, and visitors can see the entire Tumkur district from the hilltop. DD Hills is also home to several small waterfalls that are a popular spot for picnics and day trips. Visitors can take a dip in the cool waters of the waterfalls and enjoy the natural beauty of the region. The hill station is also a popular destination for rock climbing and rappelling. The hill station has several rocky outcrops that are ideal for rock climbing and rappelling. Visitors can engage in these activities under the supervision of experienced instructors. Overall, DD Hills is a great place to visit for those seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The hill station offers visitors a chance to connect with nature, explore the region's rich history and culture, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. With its picturesque location, ancient temples, and tranquil atmosphere, DD Hills is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Tumkur.
